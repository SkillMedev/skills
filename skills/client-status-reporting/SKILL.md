# Client Status Reporting

Clients do not churn because the work was bad; they churn because they could not see the work at all. "What am I paying for?" is never really a question — it is the announcement that invisibility has already done its damage, and by then the renewal is in doubt. The weekly status report is the cheapest retention instrument a freelancer owns: fifteen minutes that preempts check-in pings, forces decisions out of clients on a schedule, and makes the invoice feel obvious instead of debatable.

In the Freelancer / Consultant OS pack, this skill takes over from client-onboarding-system at day 14 and runs for the life of the engagement. Worked example: Maya, a freelance brand designer on a $4,900/month Brand System Retainer (up to 36 hours) with a DTC hot-sauce brand.

## Operating procedure

### Step 1: Collect the inputs (once, at setup)

1. The communication contract from client-onboarding-system: channel, recipient(s), and send day. Default: same weekday every week (Friday morning or Monday morning), in the engagement's single async channel.
2. The SOW deliverables and, for retainers, the monthly hours cap — the budget block reports against these.
3. Where hours are tracked. If they are not, start now; the budget block cannot be guessed. Label any estimated figure as an estimate.

### Step 2: Write the 5-block report

Five blocks, fixed order, every week. The order is deliberate: value first (done), momentum second (next), then the two blocks that transfer responsibility (blocked, decisions), and money last, where it reads as stewardship rather than alarm.

1. **DONE** — shipped this week, phrased as outcomes with links or attachments. "8 social templates delivered (link)" not "worked on templates". Never empty: a light week still produced something (research, revisions in flight, coordination) — say what and why.
2. **NEXT** — commitments for the coming week, specific enough to be checkable against next week's DONE block. This is the accountability loop that builds trust.
3. **BLOCKED** — what is waiting on the client, who owns it, since when, and what it delays. This block is the polite paper trail: when a deadline slips because assets arrived three weeks late, the record already exists in the client's own inbox, in neutral language.
4. **DECISIONS NEEDED** — each with the options, a recommendation, and a respond-by date. A decision listed without a recommendation is homework; with one, it is a 30-second reply.
5. **BUDGET / HOURS** — hours used vs. cap (retainers) or budget consumed vs. total (projects), plus any change orders in flight. Surfacing this weekly is what makes scope-creep-defense conversations easy: the 80-percent-of-hours flag lands in a block the client already reads, never as a surprise.

### Step 3: Enforce the 15-minute time cap

The report must cost 15 minutes or it will not survive contact with a busy month — and a skipped report during a heavy week is maximum damage, because heavy weeks are exactly when clients most need visibility. Hitting the cap requires logging as you go: keep a running note of shipped items and blockers during the week, then assemble on send day. Writing from memory is what turns 15 minutes into 90. Hard rules: one page maximum, bullets not prose, links instead of embedded explanations, no design or formatting effort.

### Step 4: Send on schedule, even when nothing moved

The report ships every week, same day, including vacation weeks (short version, sent early) and stall weeks. A missed report teaches the client the channel is unreliable, and unreliable reporting reads as unreliable work. Two consecutive weeks where DONE is genuinely thin is not a reporting problem — it is a delivery or blocking problem; if it is blockers, escalate the BLOCKED block into a direct conversation.

## Template: weekly status report

```
STATUS — [FILL: client] — week of [FILL: date]

DONE
- [FILL: shipped item + link]
- [FILL: shipped item + link]

NEXT (by [FILL: next report date])
- [FILL: specific commitment]
- [FILL: specific commitment]

BLOCKED — waiting on you
- [FILL: item] — owner: [FILL], since [FILL: date], delays [FILL: what]
  (or: Nothing — all clear.)

DECISIONS NEEDED
- [FILL: decision]: Option A [FILL] / Option B [FILL].
  My recommendation: [FILL]. Need your call by [FILL: date].
  (or: None this week.)

BUDGET / HOURS
- [FILL: X] of [FILL: cap] hours used this month ([FILL]%). [FILL: on track /
  flag + proposed action]. Change orders in flight: [FILL / none].
```

## Worked example: Maya's week-3 report

```
STATUS — Hot Sauce Co — week of Mar 17

DONE
- Packaging concept v2 for the Habanero SKU — 3 directions (Figma link)
- 4 of 8 March social templates delivered (folder link)
- Brand consistency audit fixes applied to Instagram grid

NEXT (by Mar 24)
- Remaining 4 March social templates
- Packaging v2 revisions once your consolidated comments land

BLOCKED — waiting on you
- Product photography for the web refresh — owner: Dana, since Mar 5,
  delays the homepage hero mockups

DECISIONS NEEDED
- Habanero packaging direction: A (bold type) / B (illustrated) / C (photo-led).
  My recommendation: B — strongest shelf differentiation vs. the two
  competitors from the kickoff brief. Need your call by Mar 21 to hold
  the print timeline.

BUDGET / HOURS
- 24 of 36 hours used (67%). On track. Change orders in flight: none.
```

Ten minutes to write from her running log. The client replies "B, and chasing Dana today" — a decision extracted, a blocker cleared, and $4,900 of monthly value made visible, in one email.

## Concrete thresholds

- Weekly, same weekday, every week of the engagement — no gaps.
- 15-minute cap, one page maximum.
- Every DECISIONS item carries a recommendation and a respond-by date.
- Hours flagged in the budget block at 80 percent of cap with more than a week left (the same threshold scope-creep-defense uses).
- A blocker appearing 2 weeks running escalates from the report to a direct conversation.

## Deliverable

The recurring one-page 5-block report, sent on schedule — plus, at renewal time, the accumulated stack of them, which doubles as the strongest possible renewal case: a written record of everything shipped, every decision made, and every delay's true owner.

## Do NOT

- Do not narrate activity ("worked on", "made progress on") — report outcomes with evidence.
- Do not soften or omit the BLOCKED block to avoid seeming pushy; unsent blockers become the freelancer's fault at deadline time.
- Do not list decisions without recommendations; open-ended questions stall for weeks, recommendations get same-day replies.
- Do not skip the report on bad weeks; silence during a stall is when "what am I paying for?" gets composed.
- Do not let the report balloon into a memo — past one page, it stops being read, and an unread report protects no one.

## Quality bar

A report passes when: all five blocks are present (with explicit "none" where empty); every DONE item has a link or artifact; NEXT is checkable against the following week; each decision has a recommendation and date; the hours figure is real, not estimated — and it took no more than 15 minutes.
