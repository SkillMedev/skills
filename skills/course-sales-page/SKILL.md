# Course Sales Page

A course sales page fails one way above all others: it sells the course instead of the transformation - modules, hours of video, and features, when the buyer only cares what their life looks like after. This skill produces the long-form page in the section order that converts cold-to-warm readers, anchors the price with a value stack, and answers objections before the buyer bounces to "maybe later" - which for course launches means never.

This skill runs after course-outline-architect, which supplies the transformation statement and the cut-list bonuses, and alongside launch-email-sequence, which drives the traffic and must quote this page's promise, stack, and guarantee word-for-word. Testimonials arrive over time from community-engagement-playbook; the page ships with what proof exists today and gets stronger every cohort.

Worked example throughout: Dana, a productivity consultant with an 8,000-subscriber list, selling "Notion OS for Consultants" at $297.

## Operating procedure

### Step 1: Collect the inputs

1. The transformation statement from course-outline-architect. If it does not exist, stop and build the outline first - a sales page for an undefined transformation is decoration.
2. The module list with milestone names, and the cut-list items destined for the bonus vault.
3. All available proof: student results, client results, the creator's own numbers, screenshots. Label anything unverified as unverified and do not use it.
4. Price, and any payment plan.
5. The top 5 to 7 objections. If unknown, mine replies from the pre-launch runway emails (launch-email-sequence collects these) or ask 5 audience members directly.

### Step 2: Write above the fold

The formula: outcome + timeframe + differentiator, then one subhead of specificity, then the primary call to action. The headline states the after, never the mechanism.

Good/bad pair for Dana:

```
BAD:  "Notion OS for Consultants - 6 modules and 28 video lessons
       on building your consulting workspace in Notion"
       (sells the course: features, format, effort)

GOOD: "Run your entire consulting practice from one Notion system -
       and onboard your next client in under a day"
       Subhead: "The exact operating system Dana built running a
       productivity consultancy - installed in 6 milestones, about
       4 hours of lessons, working from module one."
       (sells the after; the course is just the vehicle)
```

### Step 3: Write the body in this order - PAS, proof, program, price, guarantee, FAQ

The order is the argument. Do not rearrange it.

1. **Problem.** The before state in the reader's own words: six tools, docs rebuilt from scratch, roughly 5 hours a week lost to admin.
2. **Agitate.** The cost of staying: 5 hours a week is over 250 billable hours a year; at Dana's students' typical $150/hour, about $37,500 of unbilled time - plus the client who churned because onboarding felt chaotic. Use real math from the reader's world, never manufactured doom.
3. **Solve.** Introduce the transformation statement from course-outline-architect, near verbatim. Name the mechanism once, then return to outcomes.
4. **Proof.** Results before credentials: student and client outcomes with names and specifics, then Dana's own numbers, then credentials last. Only verified claims.
5. **Program.** The modules as milestones - "by the end of Module 2, every lead and proposal lives in one view" - never as a table of contents. One line per module, outcome-phrased.
6. **Price** with the value stack (Step 4).
7. **Guarantee** (Step 5).
8. **FAQ** (Step 6), then a final call to action restating the promise and the deadline.

### Step 4: Present the price with a value stack

Never let the price appear before value has been stacked. List each component with an honest standalone value, total it, then present the price against the total. Dana's stack - shared verbatim with launch-email-sequence:

```
The full Notion OS for Consultants curriculum (6 modules)   $997
12 client-ready Notion templates, yours to rebrand          $297
Bonus: Notion API automations mini-course                   $197
Private student community + weekly office hours (1 year)    $197
                                             Total value  $1,688
                                             Your price     $297
```

Value-stack rules: every line must be defensible (the templates sell separately at $297 - that is a real number, not an invention); total value between 3x and 7x price - under 3x the anchor is weak, over about 7x it reads as fake; 3 to 6 lines, because stacks of ten junk bonuses signal desperation.

### Step 5: Design the guarantee - 30 days, action-based

The default: a 30-day action-based guarantee. Dana's: "Complete Modules 1 and 2 and build your dashboard. If it hasn't saved you real time within 30 days, email your workspace link for a full refund." Action-based beats no-questions-asked for courses because it filters refund-seeking browsers, pushes buyers into the material (which drives the completion rate this pack designs for - 35 percent or better, against the 10 to 15 percent industry average), and is stronger copy: the creator is betting on the student doing the work. State it plainly; a guarantee buried in small print removes no risk.

### Step 6: Map the FAQ to objections

Each FAQ entry is a named objection wearing a question mask. Cover at minimum: time ("I'm too busy" - cite the 5-12 minute lessons and the 20-minute module-1 quick win), fit ("Will this work for MY niche"), skill ("I'm not technical"), timing ("Can I buy later" - answer honestly with the deadline and what lapses), price ("Why $297"), and access/logistics. 6 to 9 entries; answer each in under 100 words, restating a benefit.

### Step 7: Assemble from the skeleton

```
[FILL: HEADLINE - outcome + timeframe + differentiator]
[FILL: subhead - one line of specificity]        [CTA button]

PROBLEM   [FILL: before state, reader's words, 2-3 paragraphs]
AGITATE   [FILL: cost of staying, real math]
SOLVE     [FILL: transformation statement + mechanism, once]
PROOF     [FILL: results w/ names -> creator numbers -> credentials]
PROGRAM   M1-M[N]: [FILL: one outcome line per module]  [CTA]
PRICE     [FILL: value stack, 3-6 lines, total 3-7x price]
          [FILL: price + payment plan if any]
GUARANTEE [FILL: 30-day action-based, plainly worded]
FAQ       [FILL: 6-9 objection-mapped entries]
CLOSE     [FILL: restate promise + deadline]  [CTA]
```

## Deliverable

A complete long-form sales page: above-the-fold promise block, PAS opening, proof section using only verified claims, milestone-phrased program section, value-stacked price presentation, a plainly worded 30-day action-based guarantee, and a 6-to-9-entry objection-mapped FAQ - with the promise, stack, and guarantee phrased identically to what launch-email-sequence will quote.

## Do NOT

- Do not lead with modules, lesson counts, or hours of video. Features are the vehicle; the reader buys the destination.
- Do not show the price before the value stack. An unanchored $297 is expensive; against a defensible $1,688 it is obvious.
- Do not inflate stack values past roughly 7x price or pad with junk bonuses - readers smell it and the whole page loses credibility.
- Do not use unverified or composite testimonials, ever. Thin real proof beats thick fake proof, and community-engagement-playbook will supply more each cohort.
- Do not bury the guarantee or hedge it with dense conditions. One action condition, 30 days, full refund.
- Do not write FAQ entries for questions nobody asked while skipping the price and time objections everyone has.

## Quality bar

- The headline passes the "after, not vehicle" test: it would still make sense if the course were delivered by carrier pigeon.
- Sections appear in PAS → proof → program → price → guarantee → FAQ order with a CTA at minimum after the fold, after the program, and at the close.
- Every proof claim is verifiable; every stack line defensible; stack total 3-7x price.
- The guarantee names one action, one window (30 days), one outcome.
- Promise, stack, and guarantee wording matches launch-email-sequence exactly.

## Escalation and routing

Traffic and the open-cart sequence: launch-email-sequence. A webinar-driven pitch instead of a page-first launch: webinar-funnel-builder. Non-course landing pages: landing-page-copy. Collecting the testimonials this page needs: testimonial-capture-interview, prompted at the harvest moments community-engagement-playbook defines. Income-claim marketing has legal exposure in many jurisdictions - if results claims imply earnings, have counsel review; this is not legal advice.
