# Local Referral Engine

Referrals are the best customers a local service business gets: near-zero
acquisition cost, pre-sold trust, and higher close rates than any paid
channel. The costly mistake this skill prevents is leaving referrals passive
— "we get word of mouth" — instead of building the three systems that
manufacture them: a timed ask, a two-sided incentive, and a partner network.
Run local-unit-economics first to see the prize: Summit Plumbing (4 techs,
$450 average ticket, $75,000 a month) pays about $119 to acquire a paid-lead
customer, so every referred customer is roughly $119 of margin that paid
channels never touch.

## Operating procedure

### Step 1: Ask at the five-star moment — and only then

The ask-after-five-star-moment rule: the referral ask fires when a customer
has just expressed delight — a five-star review posted (review-engine detects
exactly this), an effusive thank-you at job completion, a "you saved us"
text. Never ask a merely-satisfied customer; a referral is the customer
spending social capital with a friend, and they only spend it at an
emotional peak. This is the concrete handoff from review-engine: every
five-star review triggers the referral SMS within a day or two.

```
Referral ask SMS (1-2 days after a five-star moment):
"Hi [FILL: name], [FILL: owner/tech] from Summit Plumbing — thanks
again for the kind words! Small favor: most of our work comes from
neighbors recommending us. If a friend ever needs a plumber, send
them our way — and as a thank-you, you'll both get $50 off your
next service. Just have them mention your name."
```

### Step 2: Design the incentive two-sided

Two-sided beats one-sided: reward the referrer and discount the friend. A
one-sided reward makes the recommendation feel like a commission ("is he
recommending them for the fifty bucks?"); two-sided lets the referrer open
with a gift ("use these guys, you'll get $50 off"), which preserves the
social framing that makes referrals work. Summit's design: $50 service
credit to the referrer, $50 off the friend's first job. Against a $450
average ticket at 55 percent margin, a $100 total incentive still lands the
customer far below the $119 paid CAC — and the referred customer closes
better and refers onward. Size the incentive at roughly 10-25 percent of
average ticket total across both sides. Keep redemption dead simple: "have
them mention your name," tracked in a spreadsheet — no codes, no portal.

### Step 3: Build complementary-trade partnerships

Trades see each other's future customers constantly: the electrician spots
the corroded water heater, the GC needs a plumber on every remodel, and the
plumber sees breaker panels. Build a bench of 3-5 non-competing partners —
for Summit: an electrician, a GC, an HVAC company, a restoration company.
Rules that keep it alive:

- Reciprocity is the currency. Track referrals given and received per
  partner each quarter; a partner who receives five and sends zero gets a
  friendly conversation, then gets dropped.
- Refer only businesses you would let into your own house — their bad job
  becomes your bad review.
- Money between trades: a flat thank-you referral fee ($25-50) or pure
  reciprocity both work; keep it simple and consistent. Check local
  licensing rules — some jurisdictions and trades restrict paid referrals.
- Make the intro warm: the partner texts the customer your name, not just
  the reverse. A warm handoff closes at multiples of a cold mention.
- These relationships also earn the real local links local-seo-playbook
  wants — a "trusted partners" mention on each other's sites is prominence
  that money cannot buy cleanly.

### Step 4: Dominate the neighborhood around every job

Every job is a beachhead; density is the goal, because five jobs on one
street cuts drive time and makes the brand ambient.

- Job-site signage: a small yard sign during multi-day jobs, with
  permission ("mind if we leave this up while we work?"). A truck wrapped
  with the name and number is signage that never comes down.
- "We just worked on your street" cards: after each job, the tech drops
  cards at the 10-15 nearest doors. Script on the card: "We just fixed a
  water heater for one of your neighbors on [FILL: street]. If your
  plumbing ever acts up, we're already in the neighborhood — $25 off for
  [FILL: street] residents this month." Neighbor proof plus a local-only
  offer outperforms any cold flyer.
- Neighborhood groups: when a customer offers to post in the neighborhood
  Facebook group or on Nextdoor, say yes and make it easy — a
  recommendation there is a referral to three hundred neighbors at once.

### Step 5: Track referral share monthly

Log every new customer's source at booking ("How did you hear about us?" is
mandatory, not optional). Track referral plus partner share of new
customers monthly and feed the repeat/referral rate into
local-unit-economics. Healthy mature share for a residential trade: 30-50
percent of new customers from referral, partner, and neighborhood sources.
Below 15 percent after six months of running this engine means the asks are
not actually firing — audit whether the five-star trigger is being executed,
not whether customers "just don't refer."

## Inputs to elicit

Collect: current referral share of new customers (label the usual guess as
a guess — booking-source logging is step zero), average ticket and margin
(from local-unit-economics), review flow (is review-engine live — this
skill's trigger depends on it), candidate partner trades already known, and
jobs per month per neighborhood. Defaults: $50/$50 two-sided credit, cards
at 10-15 doors per job, 3-5 partners.

## Concrete thresholds

- Ask timing: within 1-2 days of a five-star moment; never cold, never in
  the same message as the review ask (one ask per message, always).
- Incentive: two-sided, totaling 10-25 percent of average ticket; credits
  beat cash for repeat-visit trades because they guarantee a next booking.
- Partners: 3-5 active; reciprocity reviewed quarterly; give-receive
  imbalance beyond 3:1 for two quarters means drop or renegotiate.
- Neighborhood cards: 10-15 doors per completed job, same day.
- Referral share target: 30-50 percent of new customers within a year;
  under 15 percent after six months means the trigger is not firing.

## Worked artifact: Summit's referral math

```
Program: $50 credit to referrer + $50 off friend's first job
Five-star moments/mo (from review-engine, at its 12/mo target
  plus in-person delight):            ~18 asks sent
Referrals landed (25% of asks):       ~4-5 new customers/mo
Partner bench (electrician, GC, HVAC, restoration):
  referrals received:                 ~4/mo   given: ~5/mo (healthy)
Street cards: ~55 jobs/mo x 12 doors = ~660 cards -> ~3 jobs/mo
New referred/partner/neighbor customers: ~11-12/mo of ~35 new
  customers = ~32% referral share  (target band: 30-50%)
Cost check: ~$100 incentive per referred customer vs $119 paid CAC,
with a customer who closes better and refers onward. Verdict: scale.
```

## Deliverable

A referral operating system on one page: the five-star-trigger ask SMS with
fields filled, the two-sided incentive spec and its tracking sheet, a
partner bench list with the reciprocity ledger, the street-card copy and
drop routine, and the monthly referral-share line that feeds
local-unit-economics.

## Do NOT

- Do not ask merely-satisfied customers for referrals; a lukewarm referral
  spends trust the customer does not have, and the silence teaches staff
  the program "doesn't work."
- Do not run one-sided incentives; paying the referrer while the friend
  pays full price converts a recommendation into a commission.
- Do not reward referrals for leaving reviews or blend the two asks —
  review incentives violate platform policy (review-engine owns that
  boundary); referral incentives are fine, so keep the programs separate.
- Do not partner with a trade you have not seen work; their callback
  becomes your one-star review.
- Do not canvass streets cold with generic flyers; the card works because
  of the neighbor-proof line, which requires an actual nearby job.

## Quality bar

- Every ask traces to a logged five-star moment; no cold blasts.
- Both sides of the incentive are stated in every ask message.
- The partner ledger shows given and received per partner per quarter.
- Booking staff log a source for 100 percent of new customers — the share
  number is only as real as the logging.

## Escalation and routing

Check local licensing and consumer rules before paying inter-trade referral
fees — some trades and jurisdictions restrict them; this is not legal
advice. Route the five-star detection and review asks to review-engine,
partner-site links into local-seo-playbook, gym-specific referral programs
to referral-and-affiliate-system, and the monthly referral-share and
incentive-cost math into local-unit-economics to confirm the program stays
cheaper than paid acquisition.
