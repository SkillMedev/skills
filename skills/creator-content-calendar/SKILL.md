# Creator Content Calendar

A content calendar is a sustainability tool, not an idea list. Its job is to prevent decision fatigue, guarantee pillar balance, and make consistency achievable at the creator's real energy level — because the channel-killer is not bad content, it is the boom-bust cycle where an ambitious schedule collapses after three weeks and the creator disappears for two months. This skill produces a calendar structure the creator can hold through their worst week.

## Operating procedure

Run in order: cadence decisions made before pillars exist produce a schedule with nothing to fill it, and idea banks built before pillars produce ideas with no home.

### Step 1: gather inputs

1. Real weekly hours available for content, on a bad week — not an inspired one. This is the binding constraint.
2. Fixed constraints: job, family, travel rhythm, energy patterns (batch filmer vs. daily writer).
3. Primary channel and format (the calendar is single-creator; multi-channel derivatives come from repurposing, not extra slots).
4. Current output history: what they actually shipped in the last 8 weeks, not what they intended. Label any estimate a guess.
5. Goal per pillar if known (growth, trust, sales); default to growth.

### Step 2: define three to four content pillars

A pillar is not a topic; it is a content type with a predictable format and audience expectation. The standard four: **Tutorial** (how-to, educational), **Story** (experience, case study), **Opinion** (take, commentary), **Showcase** (product, result, before/after). Every piece of content must map to exactly one pillar. Hard rule: more than four pillars means no pillars — collapse until at most four remain.

### Step 3: set cadence at the consistent floor

Cadence is set by the creator's floor, not their best-week ceiling: if they can produce two pieces per week every week without exception, the cadence is two per week — even if they occasionally could do five. The right cadence is the one that survives a bad week. Proven sustainable structures:

- One long-form per week + 2–3 short-form repurposes of it.
- One long-form every two weeks + daily short-form.
- Three mid-form pieces per week, no short-form.

If stated ambition exceeds the 8-week shipping history by more than 50%, set the calendar to history plus one piece, and let them earn the raise after four consistent weeks.

### Step 4: rotate pillars on a fixed schedule

Assign pillars to slots so "what do I post today" is never a question. A four-pillar channel publishing twice weekly runs a two-week rotation: week one covers pillars one and two; week two covers pillars three and four — every pillar gets coverage monthly by construction. Theme weeks (one topic across all pillars) layer on top for launches or seasonal moments, without breaking the rotation underneath.

### Step 5: build the 30-day idea bank

Before the calendar goes live, generate 8–10 specific ideas per pillar. The bank kills the blank-page problem and enables batching — filming or writing multiple pieces in one session — which is the single biggest lever for consistency. Operating rule: the creator is never fewer than 30 days ahead on ideation (not production).

### Step 6: install the monthly audit

A 20-minute audit every month: what published, what did not, and why. Track exactly one metric per pillar (views, shares, or replies — whichever matches that pillar's goal) to find which pillar grows the audience fastest. Adjust pillar weight quarterly, on data, not preference. A missed post is not a failure; it is a cadence recalibration signal — if two posts slip in a month, the floor was set too high, so lower the cadence rather than shame the creator toward quitting.

## Weekly calendar skeleton

Copy and fill. Example rotation: 4 pillars, 2 posts/week, two-week cycle.

```
CREATOR: [FILL]   CADENCE: [FILL: n]/week (floor-verified)   HOURS/WK: [FILL]
PILLARS: 1.[FILL: e.g. Tutorial] 2.[FILL: Story] 3.[FILL: Opinion] 4.[FILL: Showcase]

WEEK A
  Slot 1 — [FILL: day]  | Pillar 1 | Idea: [FILL from bank] | Batch day: [FILL]
  Slot 2 — [FILL: day]  | Pillar 2 | Idea: [FILL from bank] | Batch day: [FILL]
WEEK B
  Slot 1 — [FILL: day]  | Pillar 3 | Idea: [FILL from bank] | Batch day: [FILL]
  Slot 2 — [FILL: day]  | Pillar 4 | Idea: [FILL from bank] | Batch day: [FILL]
(rotation repeats: A, B, A, B…)

IDEA BANK (8-10 per pillar before launch)
  Pillar 1: [FILL x8-10]   Pillar 2: [FILL x8-10]
  Pillar 3: [FILL x8-10]   Pillar 4: [FILL x8-10]

MONTHLY AUDIT (20 min, [FILL: recurring date])
  Shipped vs planned: ____/____   Slipped because: [FILL]
  Pillar metrics — P1 [FILL metric]: __  P2: __  P3: __  P4: __
  Fastest-growing pillar: ____   Quarterly weight change (data only): [FILL]
```

Detail-plan only the next four weeks; week five onward stays as bare pillar slots, because over-planning is the other way calendars die.

## Deliverable

Produce a calendar system containing: the 3–4 named pillars with format and audience expectation each, the floor-verified cadence with its justification, the fixed rotation mapped onto the weekly skeleton, a launched 30-day idea bank (8–10 ideas per pillar), and the monthly audit template with one metric assigned per pillar.

## Do NOT

- Do not build the calendar around an ideal scenario; build around the job, family, and energy constraints stated in Step 1 — an aspirational calendar is a quitting plan with dates on it.
- Do not allow more than four pillars; a channel that is about everything is discovered for nothing.
- Do not set cadence from a motivated week; the ceiling always collapses, and the collapse costs more than the extra posts gained.
- Do not detail-plan beyond four weeks out; leave week five onward as pillar slots only.
- Do not fill slots with ideas that map to no pillar — that is how calendars drift back into random posting.
- Do not treat a missed post as failure; treat two misses in a month as a signal to lower the floor.

## Quality bar

- Every slot in the skeleton maps to exactly one pillar, and every pillar appears at least monthly by construction.
- The cadence is defensible against the creator's worst realistic week and their actual 8-week history.
- The idea bank holds 8–10 ideas per pillar before the calendar starts.
- The audit has a date, a 20-minute scope, and one named metric per pillar.
- The creator could execute the next four weeks without making a single "what should I post" decision.

For a brand or team running multiple channels with campaign dates and per-channel cadences, route to social-content-calendar. Once a pillar piece ships, content-repurposing fills the short-form slots from it.
